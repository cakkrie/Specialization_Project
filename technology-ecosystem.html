Pre-Fishing - Preparation & Planning


Inputs: User profile (experience level, preferences), Time availability, Location access, Weather data, Regulations

Agent Actions: Collect user goals ("I want to fish this weekend for 3 hours."), Analyze external data (weather, water level, seasonality), Recommend optimal time & location for fishing, Suggest equipment & bait (with purchase/rental links), Provide a step-by-step preparation checklist

(02)

During Fishing - Real-Time Guidance & Support


Inputs: GPS location, Live conditions (weather/water APIs), Manual user updates

Agent Actions: Provide real-time tips (e.g., adjust bait, change spot), Offer safety alerts for sudden weather/water risks, Enable quick-reference tutorials (casting, knot tying), Track catches & efforts automatically (photo or manual logging), Offer motivational nudges if no fish caught after a while

(03)

Post-Fishing - Reflection & Engagement


Inputs: Catch log, Effort data, Social sharing intent

Agent Actions: Summarize trip results (e.g., effort vs. outcome), Suggest improvements for next time (learning loop), Provide AI-generated trip journal (story + photos), Enable social sharing (successes & learnings), Offer gamification: badges, streaks, community challenges

Related Technology


(01)
RAG
/

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) combines search and generation — it retrieves relevant information from a database, then uses an AI model to create accurate, natural answers grounded in real data.

  • Step 1: Retrieve Relevant Data: When a user asks a question, the system searches a fishing database that includes guides, species information, bait recommendations, user catch logs, and local regulations. Using vector search and embeddings, it identifies the most relevant pieces of information related to the query, ensuring the AI works with accurate, context-specific data.
  • Step 2 – Generate a Smart, Contextual Answer:The language model then reads the retrieved information and generates a clear, natural response tailored to the user’s question. For example, it might suggest the best bait or fishing time based on recent catches and water conditions — producing advice that’s grounded in real fishing data rather than general knowledge.
(02)
AI Feature Extraction
/

AI feature extraction is the process of automatically identifying and analyzing key patterns or signals from raw data — such as images, maps, weather data, or sonar readings — to reveal meaningful insights. Instead of humans manually studying every variable, AI models detect features like water temperature changes, vegetation density, underwater terrain, current flow, or fish movement patterns, and turn them into usable indicators.
For anglers, feature extraction can help identify better fishing spots by analyzing data from satellite imagery, sonar scans, catch history, and environmental sensors. For example, the AI might detect areas with the right combination of depth, structure, and temperature where a specific fish species tends to gather. By learning from past catches and environmental features, the system can highlight high-probability fishing zones on a map — helping users save time, plan smarter trips, and increase their chances of success..

An IoT fishing device can collect real-time underwater data — such as sonar signals, water temperature, turbidity, pressure, or even acoustic patterns of fish movement.
